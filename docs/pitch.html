The Insight

The behavioral skeleton of phishing never changes.
It can't. Because it's not a property of the attack —
it's a property of the scam.

Every phishing attack in history follows the same shape: unexpected arrival, artificial urgency, identity claim, credential request. The specifics are infinite. The shape is invariant. Attackers can change every pixel, every domain, every line of code. They cannot change the shape, because the shape IS the scam.

Virgil stops detecting fakes and starts detecting the shape.
